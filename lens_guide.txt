### PHASE 1: DISCOVERY 
*Extract raw data and identify human friction.*

**The Signal Extractor:** Translates raw ramblings into business kernels.
> Use this persona to strip emotional fluff and validate if an unstructured idea actually contains a monetizable core or just empty buzzwords.

**User Advocate:** Maps emotional needs and hidden friction.
> Useful for identifying why users will actually care, finding the psychological friction in your workflows, and avoiding feature bloat.

---

### PHASE 2: DEFINITION 
*Structure the product and determine feasibility.*

**User Experience Architect:** IA/IxD, and cognitive load.
> Use this persona to map out the exact screens, user journeys, and information architecture required to make the product functionally usable.

**Product Manager:** MVP and JTBD prioritization.
> Useful for cutting scope, defining the critical path for launch, and ensuring every feature directly serves a core Job-To-Be-Done.

**Technical Lead:** Feasibility and scaling.
> Use this persona to audit tech stacks, identify engineering bottlenecks, and prevent you from designing features you cannot afford to build.

---

### PHASE 3: STRESS-TESTING & RECONSTRUCTION 
*Break the logic before the market does, then rebuild it.*

**The Devil’s Advocate:** Fatal flaws via logic/market failure.
> Useful for actively trying to destroy your business model. It finds the unit economic holes and logical fallacies that will kill the product.

**The Solutions Architect:** Rebuilds broken ideas into viable frameworks.
> Use this persona right after the Devil's Advocate to take the shattered pieces of your strategy and reassemble them into a model that works.

**Business Strategist:** ROI and competitive moats.
> Useful for defining pricing models, evaluating market positioning, and ensuring your product has a defensible moat against larger competitors.

---

### PHASE 4: DISTRIBUTION 
*Get it to the user.*

**Growth Marketer:** Distribution and Acquisition logic.
> Use this persona to architect deterministic viral loops, define CAC/LTV mechanics, and build a measurable go-to-market acquisition engine.
